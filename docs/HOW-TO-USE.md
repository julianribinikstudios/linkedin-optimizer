# LinkedIn Optimizer — How to Use

A Cowork skill that rewrites your LinkedIn profile end-to-end and updates it directly on the site for you. It walks you through your headline, About section, experience descriptions, and skills, with you approving every change before it goes live.

Works for any industry or goal — whether you're trying to attract clients, land a new job, get speaking gigs, or just look more professional online.

---

## What you'll need before you start

1. **Cowork installed and running.** Cowork is Anthropic's desktop app that powers this skill. If you don't have it yet, get it from [claude.com](https://claude.com).

2. **The Claude in Chrome extension.** This is what actually clicks around your LinkedIn page. Install it from the Chrome Web Store (search for "Claude in Chrome") and sign in. The skill won't work without it.

3. **Chrome open with LinkedIn loaded.** Make sure you're logged into your LinkedIn account in Chrome before you start. The skill operates on whichever account is currently logged in.

4. **30-60 minutes of focused time.** This isn't a 5-minute job. A full optimization with thoughtful answers to the discovery questions and careful review of each section takes about an hour. You can also do it in chunks across multiple sessions.

---

## Step 1: Install the skill

1. Download the `linkedin-optimizer.skill` file (someone should have sent it to you, or you grabbed it from a link).

2. Double-click the file. Cowork should open and show an install prompt.

3. Click **Save skill** in the prompt.

That's it — the skill is now available in your Cowork.

---

## Step 2: Start a conversation in Cowork

Open Cowork and start a new chat. Then type something like:

- "Help me optimize my LinkedIn profile"
- "Can you rewrite my LinkedIn?"
- "I want to make my LinkedIn better for attracting [clients / job offers / partnerships]"
- "Run the LinkedIn optimizer on my profile"

Any of these will trigger the skill. You don't have to use the exact name.

---

## Step 3: Let it read your current profile

The skill will start by navigating to your LinkedIn profile in Chrome and reading what's there. You'll see screenshots and a summary of what it found:

- Your current headline
- Your current About section (or a note if it's empty)
- How many experience entries you have
- How many skills are listed

If you don't see Chrome opening or it can't reach LinkedIn, double-check that the Claude in Chrome extension is installed and you're logged in.

---

## Step 4: Answer the discovery questions

Before rewriting anything, the skill will ask you 5-6 questions to understand your goals. **These answers shape every single word it writes**, so take a minute to actually think about them:

1. **What's your industry and role?** Be specific. Not "I work in tech" — say "fractional CFO for early-stage startups" or "freelance wedding photographer in Brooklyn."

2. **What are you trying to attract?** Clients? Job offers? Partnerships? Speaking gigs? Pick whatever is true. You can pick multiple.

3. **Any positioning to avoid?** Words, phrases, or framings you don't want. For example: "Don't call me a freelancer" or "Avoid the word guru."

4. **Credentials and proof points?** Press features, awards, certifications, notable clients, years of experience — anything that builds credibility.

5. **Markets and locations?** Where do you serve clients or want to work? Be specific (e.g., "NYC and the Tri-State area" or "remote globally" or "Bay Area only").

6. **What makes you different?** One sentence. The thing only you (or few people) can say truthfully.

The more detail you give here, the better the rewrite will be. Vague inputs produce generic outputs.

---

## Step 5: Review and approve each section

The skill will rewrite your profile in order: headline → About → experience entries → skills → keyword pass. **It will show you proposed copy before updating anything on LinkedIn.** Nothing goes live without your approval.

### For the headline
You'll get 3-5 different options. They'll each have a character count next to them. Read through and either:
- Pick one as-is ("Use option 2")
- Mix elements ("Use option 1 but swap in the credential from option 3")
- Ask for revisions ("Make them more focused on partnerships, less on job-seeking")

### For the About section
You'll see a full draft (about 250 words). Read it carefully. Things to check:
- Does it sound like *you*, or like a robot?
- Are there any facts that are wrong or overstated?
- Is the call-to-action what you actually want people to do?
- Did it include your email if you wanted it included (or leave it out if you didn't)?

Ask for as many revisions as you need. It's much easier to iterate here than to fix it later.

### For experience entries
You'll see all the proposed rewrites at once so you can see the big picture. The skill might also flag duplicate or empty entries and ask if you want to delete them — be deliberate about this. Deleted entries don't come back automatically.

### For skills
You'll get a list of 5-10 recommended skills. Approve, remove any that don't fit, or add ones the skill missed.

### For the keyword pass
The skill will identify high-value search terms missing from your profile and suggest where to weave them in. Make sure the additions still sound natural — if anything feels stuffed, push back.

---

## Step 6: Final review

When everything is done, you'll get a before/after summary covering every change made: headline before vs. after, what was added to the About, which experience entries got new descriptions, which skills were added, and what keywords are now searchable.

The skill will also suggest a few next steps you can do on your own:
- Ask former colleagues or clients for skill endorsements
- Request a recommendation or two
- Update your profile photo or banner if they're old
- Turn on "Open to Work" or "Providing Services" if relevant
- Post something to your network announcing the refresh

---

## Tips for getting the best result

**Be honest about your goals.** If you say "attract clients" but you're actually job hunting, the copy won't serve you. The framing for a business owner is very different from a job seeker.

**Don't accept the first draft just because it sounds good.** Read it like a stranger would. If you wouldn't reach out to this person based on their profile, ask for changes.

**Push back on jargon.** If the draft uses industry buzzwords you'd never say out loud, swap them. Authentic beats impressive.

**Don't optimize for keywords at the expense of voice.** LinkedIn SEO matters, but a profile that ranks well and sounds robotic still loses to one that ranks slightly worse but sounds like a real person.

**Do it in chunks if needed.** You don't have to finish in one sitting. You can pause after the headline, come back tomorrow, and pick up from the About section.

## Troubleshooting

**"Chrome isn't responding" or LinkedIn won't load.**
Make sure the Claude in Chrome extension is installed, signed in, and you're logged into LinkedIn in Chrome. If issues persist, restart Chrome and try again.

**"LinkedIn is showing a CAPTCHA."**
This sometimes happens after rapid automated actions. Solve the CAPTCHA manually in Chrome, then tell the skill "ok, ready to continue."

**"The edits aren't saving."**
LinkedIn occasionally has save lag. Take a screenshot to verify, and if changes really didn't stick, try the edit again.

**"It's writing copy that doesn't sound like me."**
Push back with specifics: "Too corporate, make it warmer" or "Cut the buzzwords" or "Less formal." Skip a vague "I don't like it" — tell it what to change.

**"It missed something important about my background."**
Just tell it. "Don't forget to mention my book" or "Add that I've been featured in Forbes." It'll work that in.

**"I want to start over on the headline."**
Just ask. "Give me 5 new headline options with a different angle."

## What this skill won't do

- It won't post on your behalf or send connection requests
- It won't write your recommendations or endorse people
- It won't change your profile photo or banner image
- It can't see private analytics or who viewed your profile
- It doesn't have access to your LinkedIn DMs

It's focused entirely on the public-facing copy of your profile.

---

## Privacy note

The skill operates inside your own browser session on your own LinkedIn account. It reads what's on your profile (which is public anyway), proposes changes, and makes edits you approve. It doesn't send your profile data anywhere outside the Cowork conversation, and Anthropic's standard privacy policy applies.
